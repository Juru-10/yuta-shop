African different Cultures Wedding Styles

Rwanda

As with most weddings, the attention centers around the bride. In a traditional Rwandan wedding, the bride wears Kinyarwanda, or the more commonly known Umushanana.

Egypt

Egyptian brides wear beautiful designed gowns accentuated with gold embroidery and made with linen. The bust area of the dress usually has intricate gold detailing. In some parts, the bride wears a matching headdress to cover her face.

Eritrea

The Tingrinya people call Ethiopia home. In a traditional Ethiopian wedding, the bride and groom wear a black velvet cape called a Ghaba. They wear crowns with gold embroidery to symbolize that they are the king and queen of their household. Under her cape, the bride wears a beautifully designed gown and the male under his a tailored suit

Ghana

The Ashanti tribe is from Ghana. For the wedding attires, brides and groom wear the Kente cloth, a woven fabric produced by the Ashanti people.

Nigeria

Yoruba

The wedding attire for the Yoruba bride and groom consist of layers. The first layer is a long wide-sleeved piece.

Igbo

Beautifully designed wedding attires and vibrant colors make Igbo weddings, called Igbankwu, splendid affairs
